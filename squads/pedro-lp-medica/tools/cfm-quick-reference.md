# CFM Resolution 2.336/2023 Quick Reference

> **Complete quick-reference guide to CFM advertising rules for physicians** — Used by cfm-compliance-sentinel and medical-copywriter to ensure all medical landing pages comply with Brazilian medical advertising regulations.

---

## Resolution 2.336/2023 — Key Articles

### Art. 1º — General Principles

Medical advertising must be:
- **Informative** — Primary purpose is to inform, not sell
- **Truthful** — All claims must be verifiable
- **Ethical** — Aligned with the Medical Code of Ethics
- **Professional** — Maintain dignity of the profession

### Art. 2º — Prohibited Practices

Physicians MUST NOT:

| # | Prohibition | Example Violation |
|---|-------------|-------------------|
| I | Guarantee results | "Resultado garantido em 30 dias" |
| II | Use sensationalist language | "O melhor médico do Brasil" |
| III | Display before/after photos promotionally | Before/after gallery as marketing |
| IV | Associate medical services with consumer products | "Botox promotion — buy 2, get 1" |
| V | Use patient testimonials for promotional purposes | "See what our patients say!" as main marketing |
| VI | Advertise free examinations with commercial intent | "Free consultation — sign up now" |
| VII | Use body images with erotic or sensationalist appeal | Sexualized imagery in medical context |
| VIII | Reference pricing in a commercial manner | "Cheapest rhinoplasty in São Paulo" |
| IX | Promote medical procedures as consumer products | "Black Friday: 50% off liposuction" |
| X | Self-promote as "the best," "number one," etc. | "#1 plastic surgeon" |

### Art. 3º — Permitted Content

Physicians MAY:

- [ ] Share educational content about conditions and treatments
- [ ] Inform about their specialty, credentials, and services
- [ ] Share their CRM number and specialty registration (RQE)
- [ ] Publish office hours, location, and contact information
- [ ] Participate in interviews and media appearances
- [ ] Maintain professional websites and social media
- [ ] Use before/after photos in **scientific/educational** context only
- [ ] Share technical articles and research

### Art. 4º — Social Media Rules

| Rule | Requirement |
|------|-------------|
| Identification | Must include name and CRM |
| Content | Must be educational or informational |
| Testimonials | Cannot use patient testimonials as promotion |
| Before/after | Only in scientific context, not promotional |
| Live streams | Must maintain professional standards |
| Filters | Cannot alter medical results in images |

### Art. 5º — Digital Advertising

| Element | Rule |
|---------|------|
| Website | Must identify physician + CRM |
| Landing pages | Cannot guarantee outcomes |
| Google Ads | Must comply with all CFM rules |
| Email marketing | Must be informational, not commercial |
| WhatsApp | Professional use only, no mass marketing |

## Specialty-Specific Rules

### Plastic Surgery (Most Regulated)

| Rule | Details |
|------|---------|
| Before/after photos | Only in scientific publications, not on marketing pages |
| "Natural result" claims | Cannot imply results look "natural" or "undetectable" |
| Recovery time claims | Must state individual results vary |
| Technique claims | Must use medically accurate terminology |

### Dermatology

| Rule | Details |
|------|---------|
| Product endorsements | Cannot promote specific commercial products |
| Skin results claims | Cannot guarantee specific outcomes |
| Aesthetic procedures | Must distinguish medical vs. cosmetic purpose |

### Cardiology / Internal Medicine

| Rule | Details |
|------|---------|
| Treatment claims | Must be evidence-based |
| Cure claims | Cannot promise cures for chronic conditions |
| Diagnostic claims | Cannot guarantee diagnostic accuracy |

### Psychiatry / Mental Health

| Rule | Details |
|------|---------|
| Treatment outcome claims | Cannot guarantee mental health outcomes |
| Medication discussions | Must not prescribe or recommend specific drugs |
| Testimonials | Especially restricted — patient vulnerability |

## Penalty Framework

| Severity | Penalty | Examples |
|----------|---------|----------|
| Light | Warning, fine (up to 50x ethical allowance value) | Minor infractions, first offense |
| Serious | Fine (up to 100x), public censure | Repeated infractions, misleading claims |
| Very Serious | Fine (up to 200x), suspension up to 2 years | Guaranteed results, patient harm from misinformation |
| Grave | Cessation of medical registration | Fraud, repeated severe violations |

## Compliance Checklist for Landing Pages

- [ ] Physician full name displayed
- [ ] CRM number visible
- [ ] RQE (specialty registration) if claiming specialty
- [ ] No guaranteed outcome language
- [ ] No "best," "#1," or superlative claims
- [ ] No before/after photos (unless scientific context)
- [ ] No patient testimonials as primary marketing
- [ ] No commercial pricing language
- [ ] No Black Friday / promotional event framing
- [ ] Content is educational/informational in nature
- [ ] LGPD compliance for health data (Art. 11)
- [ ] Contact information (address, phone) present
- [ ] No sensationalist or erotic imagery
- [ ] Medical terminology is accurate and current
